Contract Guidelines

A few practical notes to help set expectations before getting in touch about freelance Apple platform development.

Having worked as a freelancer for over a decade, I know what sort of business relationship works best for both me and my clients. I have a wealth of experience that means I am not just a developer, but somebody who can help you at the design, UX, development, and marketing stages. I like to get involved early in a project so I can help you avoid costly design mistakes, but also so I can get a good feel for the work that needs to be done.

I am passionate about making best-of-breed products, exemplified by the numerous #1 apps I have helped build on the App Store, and I like to be involved at every stage so I can put across best practices. For this reason, I very rarely work on projects that have been started by another developer unless starting from scratch is an option.

I work remotely from my own office and am available via video call, Slack, and email.

  • Day rate£800 per day (plus VAT if applicable), billed through Dodo Apps Ltd.
  • Project sizeShort contracts and projects are preferred. I generally do not take on work longer than 3 months unless the project is especially interesting.
  • PlatformsNative iOS, iPadOS, macOS, watchOS, tvOS, and visionOS only. I do not build native Android or cross-platform apps.

Payment Terms

My payment terms are broken down based on the total value of the project:

  • Less than 2 weeks: 50% on start, 50% on completion
  • 2-6 weeks: 25% on start, 25% at a defined midpoint, usually first prototype build, and 50% on completion
  • More than 6 weeks: negotiable, typically invoiced at the end of each calendar month for days worked during that month

All invoices are expected to be paid within 7 days. I never work on an equity or profit-share basis.

I work in a clear, iterative way, usually in 1-2 week sprints with frequent test builds so you can see progress as it happens. I am always upfront about judging how long a piece of work will take and will make suggestions on how to get the best value for your money.

You may wish to look at the apps I have built for clients or my own self-published apps to get a feel for the quality of work I do.
